Koala

Koalas are marsupials native to Australia, known for their distinctive appearance and tree-dwelling lifestyle. They have a stout, tailless body, large round ears, and a flat nose.

Koalas primarily feed on eucalyptus leaves, which are toxic to most animals. They have a specialized digestive system that allows them to detoxify the leaves.

Koalas are mostly nocturnal and spend most of their time sleeping in trees, where they can be safe from predators.

They are social animals and often live in loose colonies, communicating through vocalizations and scent markings.

Koalas are known for their unique vocalizations, which can be heard over long distances.

They are also known for their strong sense of smell, which helps them locate food and communicate with other koalas.

Koalas are threatened by habitat loss, bushfires, and disease.
