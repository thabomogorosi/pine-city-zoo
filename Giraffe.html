Giraffe

The giraffe is the tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant. It is a species of the genus Giraffa, which is part of the family Giraffidae.

Giraffes are known for their long necks, which allow them to reach high branches and foliage.

They are herbivorous and primarily feed on leaves, fruits, and flowers from trees and shrubs.

Giraffes are social animals and often form loose herds, which provide protection against predators.

They are known for their unique coat patterns, which vary among individuals and subspecies.

Giraffes are found in various habitats, including savannas, grasslands, and open woodlands.

They play a crucial role in their ecosystems by helping to maintain the balance of vegetation and providing food for other animals.

Giraffes are threatened by habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict.
