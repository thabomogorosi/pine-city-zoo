Animals Map Places

Elephant

Elephants are large mammals of the family Elephantidae and the order Proboscidea. They are the largest existing land animals. Two species are traditionally recognized, the African elephant and the Asian elephant.

Gemsbok

The gemsbok is a large antelope with long, straight horns and a distinctive black and white facial pattern. It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa and is well adapted to survive in harsh desert environments.

Giraffe

The giraffe is the tallest living terrestrial animal and the largest ruminant. It is a species of the genus Giraffa, which is part of the family Giraffidae.

Gorilla

The gorilla is a ground-dwelling, predominantly herbivorous ape that inhabits the forests of central Sub-Saharan Africa. It is the largest living primate and is classified in the genus Gorilla.

Koala

The koala is an arboreal herbivorous marsupial native to Australia. It is the only extant representative of the family Phascolarctidae, and its closest living relatives are the wombats.

Lion

The lion is a species in the family Felidae and a member of the genus Panthera. It is the second-largest living cat after the tiger. The lion is a muscular, deep-chested cat with a short, rounded head, a reduced neck, and round ears.

Monkey

Monkeys are haplorhine primates of the infraorder Simiiformes, also known as simians. They are generally considered to be a paraphyletic group, meaning that they do not include all descendants of a common ancestor.

Panda

The giant panda, also known as the panda bear or simply the panda, is a bear species classified in the genus Ailuropoda, native to south central China. It is characterized by its bold black-and-white coat and rotund body.

Tiger

The tiger is the largest species among the Felidae and classified in the genus Panthera. It is most recognizably identified by its dark vertical stripes on orange-brown fur with a lighter underside.

Warthog

The warthog is a wild member of the pig family found in sub-Saharan Africa. It is the only extant member of the genus Phacochoerus, and it is characterized by its tusks and facial warts.
