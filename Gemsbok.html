Gemsbok

The gemsbok is a large antelope with long, straight horns and a distinctive black and white facial pattern. It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa and is well adapted to survive in harsh desert environments.

Gemsboks are known for their remarkable ability to withstand extreme temperatures and can go for long periods without water.

They are social animals and often form herds, which provide protection against predators.

Gemsboks are herbivorous and primarily feed on grasses, leaves, and shrubs.

They are known for their agility and speed, which help them escape from predators such as lions and hyenas.
